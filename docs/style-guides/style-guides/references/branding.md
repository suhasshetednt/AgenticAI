## 1. Logo anatomy

The logo is comprised of **three main elements**:

1. The **ASL type unit** with small white foil (inside the "A")
2. The **large foil** (the swoosh underneath)
3. The **suffix** — "Aviation Holdings" or "Airlines" (and, for variants, a country, company or department name)

There are two principal logos:

- **ASL Aviation Holdings** — used for the group / parent company.
- **ASL Airlines** — used for the operating airlines, with a country suffix (Ireland, France, Belgium, United Kingdom, Australia, Hungary, etc.).

Both are evolutions of the previous logo, designed to feel contemporary and to convey **solidity, confidence and growth**.

All logos are supplied locked-up by ASL Corporate Affairs. The basic elements are locked and must not be redrawn.

## 3. Logo usage rules

### Sizing

- The ASL Airlines Logo can be used as large as necessary, but when reduced for print purposes, **must not be less than 30mm wide**.
- Safe area: **no less than 10mm** clear space around the basic grid of the logo.

### Colour and background

- Logos can be used **against white**, or **reversed out of dark blue, black, or images**.
- Irrespective of the background, the small white foil inside the "A" of ASL must **always be white-out** (i.e. left as background showing through).
- Take care with background usage to ensure **maximum visibility** of the logo at all times.

### Non-distortion

- The logos must not be distorted.
- None of the four elements may be changed or resized out of proportion to the others.

### Approvals

- Any use of the logo in **white or another colour** must be approved in advance by the Brand Guardian.
- Use of the logo against non-standard applications must be approved in advance by the Brand Guardian.

## 4. Linear logos

Linear logos are **single-line, horizontal** versions of the ASL logo. They exist for countries only — **linear logos are not available for companies or departments.**

Stacked (two-line) linear versions of the country logos also exist; the same rules apply to them.

### When linear logos may be used

Only **in exceptional circumstances**, where the standard stacked logo will not fit or, when reduced to fit, will not be clearly visible. The canonical example is an **airport-issued ID badge**.

### When linear logos must NOT be used

- **Not on email**, including email signatures.
- **Not on stationery** of any kind (printed or digital).
- **Not on PowerPoint or other presentations.**
- **Not on Teams or other video-call backgrounds.**
- **Not on clothing** — except with prior design approval by the Brand Guardian.
- **Not on signs** (buildings or otherwise) — only full stacked logos are permitted on signage.
- **On vehicles only** if the stacked logo will not fit in the available space.

When in doubt, use the standard stacked logo.

These are the only brand colours. **No other colours should be used at any time** except when the logo is required in white or another colour — which must be approved in advance by the Brand Guardian.

A gradient from **ASL Light Blue** (`#86D0F4`) to **ASL Dark Blue** (`#004B93`) is the standard brand gradient, used on PowerPoint title slides, section dividers, advertising backgrounds, and similar.

## 6. Typography

**Calibri 11pt is the standard font** for letters, emails, presentations, and all written communications — including digital communications.

- Larger or smaller Calibri point sizes may be used as required for presentation purposes.
- All submissions for internal communications outlets — **Weekly News, Altitude, Connexion, and the Group Intranet** — must be in **Calibri 11pt**.
- Use of other fonts may be required from time to time for presentations, digital purposes, or advertising/promotions. **This must be approved in advance by the Brand Guardian.**

The four standard Calibri weights are: Regular, Bold, Regular Italic, Bold Italic.

(Note: the logos themselves use Gotham Black Italic / Gotham Bold Italic, but these are only present in the supplied locked-up logo files — they are not typefaces used in body copy.)

## 8. Business cards

Business cards use **only** the ASL Aviation Holdings logo, the ASL Airlines logo, or the ASL Maintenance logo — as appropriate.

**Country-specific logos are NOT used on business cards.** Country names are printed on the card under the holder's name and job title.

### Front of card

Format:
```
[Logo top-right]
Name Surname
Job Title
[Entity name — e.g. "ASL Airlines Ireland"]
Tel: …
Mobile: …
Email: …
www.…
[Address block right-aligned]
```

### Rear of card

- **ASL Aviation Holdings card** — features the ASL Aviation Holdings logo prominently in the middle.
- **ASL Maintenance card** — features the ASL Maintenance logo prominently in the middle, with a small ASL Aviation Holdings logo bottom-right.
- **Country (airline) cards** — feature the ASL Airlines logo prominently in the middle, with a list of all ASL airline country logos across the bottom: **ASLI, ASLF, ASLB, ASLUK, ASLA, ASLS, ASLH**.

(Airline acronyms: ASLI = Ireland, ASLF = France, ASLB = Belgium, ASLUK = United Kingdom, ASLA = Australia, ASLS = Airline Services Switzerland, ASLH = Hungary.)

## 9. Email signatures

The ASL email signature is in a format similar to a business card. It consists of a **graphic header** containing the appropriate ASL logo, followed by name, title, and contact details underneath.

```
[Graphic header with logo]
Name Surname
Job Title | Department
[Entity — e.g. "ASL Airlines Ireland"]
T:  +xxx (0)x xxx xxxx
M:  +xxx (0)xx xxx xxxx
www.[entity-url]
```

**Rules:**

- Template email signatures are available **in each ASL airline and company**. **These are the only email signatures that should be used.**
- There should be **no additions or removal of any element** of the email signature.
- **Linear logos must not be used** on email signatures.

## 10. Identity cards

For **security and brand reasons**, all personal identification (ID) cards across all ASL companies use the same design.

**Rules:**

- Use the appropriate logo on the card front: ASL Aviation Holdings, ASL Maintenance, or a country-specific ASL Airlines logo (e.g. ASL Airlines Ireland).
- The **ASL Aviation Holdings logo, in blue, must appear on the bottom right** of all cards.
- **Crew ID cards** must have a **black-on-yellow "CREW"** notation under the photograph.
- **Emergency response team ID cards** must have a **white-on-red "EMERGENCY RESPONSE TEAM"** notation along the bottom.
- All ID cards must include an **expiry date** and **staff (employee) number**.

The card body is in ASL Light Blue, with the photo on the left and the holder's name, position, employee number, and expiry date to its right. The base of the card carries a repeating "…asl airlines…" pattern.

## 11. PowerPoint templates

The current PowerPoint background templates feature the ASL brand gradient and dotted-world-map motif. Templates exist for:

- **ASL Aviation Holdings** (used by all group departments — IT, HR, Finance, etc.)
- **ASL Airlines** (each country uses the same airline template with the appropriate country logo)
- **ASL Maintenance**
- Two **special-programme** templates: **Project Darwin** and **The ASL Way** — these may **only** be used in connection with those projects.

**Rules:**

- The ASL Aviation Holdings logo (or the relevant airline / maintenance logo) **must always appear** on the **title slide**, **end slide**, and the **bottom right of every content slide**.
- Any background changes must originate from ASL Aviation Holdings and be approved by the Brand Guardian; new ideas can be submitted at any time but **must not be used until approved**.
- All departments (Information Technology, Human Resources, etc.) should use the **ASL Aviation Holdings** PowerPoint template — not a custom departmental template.
- **Linear logos must not be used** on PowerPoint.
- Standard background and logos should remain in place; content can be added as required by each airline or company.

A standard "associates" slide variant exists that shows the logos of group associated entities (e.g. **FlySafair, Safair, K-Mile, Quikjet, X-air services**) across the top of the slide.

## 12. Vehicles

**Rules for newly acquired ASL vehicles:**

- Purchase in **all-white** or **all-navy** (as close as possible to ASL Dark Blue).
- Logos appear in the **opposite colour** — dark blue on white, white on dark blue.
- Use the **full stacked logo**: either ASL Airlines, the specific ASL country logo, or ASL Maintenance.
- If possible, apply a **smaller ASL Aviation Holdings logo** as well — desirable but not absolutely necessary.
- The only other logos permitted are those required for **airport ramp driving** and the **appropriate ASL website URL**.
- Required markings — fleet number, regulatory, security, vehicle function — should conform with local regulations.

**Linear logos on vehicles** should only be used where there is a height restriction, and should usually have Brand Guardian approval.

## 13. Buildings and signage

- All signs should be on either a **white** or **ASL Dark Blue** background.
- **White signs** feature full logos in **both dark and light blue, or dark blue only**.
- **Dark blue signs** feature **all-white logos**.
- **No other colours** should be used.
- **Only full logos** — linear or stacked-linear logos must **not** be used for signs.
- On airline or company signs, a **small ASL Aviation Holdings logo** should also be placed at the **bottom right**.

## 14. Promotional items

The brand guidelines for promotional and security items are stated to be **under review** (as of the 2023 guide). Items include mugs, pens, lanyards, clothing, hats and caps, golf umbrellas, mousepads, and similar.

**General principles:**

- Promotional items, including clothing, should be in **ASL Dark Blue, ASL Light Blue, White**, or a combination of these.
- Only **full stacked logos** should be used unless approved by the Brand Guardian.
- No other text, logos, part-logos, or pictures should be used without Brand Guardian approval. Such approval **will not be unreasonably withheld** but the item must be **complimentary to the brand and corporate reputation**.

## 15. Workwear and uniforms

The source guide shows illustrative examples rather than prescriptive rules. The visible standards are:

**Cabin crew (airlines):** Navy suit / dress / coat as the principal garment; **ASL Light Blue** scarves, blouses, and accents; navy leather gloves and pillbox hat with light-blue swoosh detail.

**Flight crew (pilots):** Navy double-breasted jacket with gold rank stripes on the sleeves; light-blue shirt; navy tie; black leather belt with embossed swoosh detail.

**Ground / maintenance staff:** Navy or royal-blue polo shirts and outerwear with the full stacked logo; hi-vis yellow safety jackets carrying the full logo in dark blue on the back and front; navy work trousers; striped neckties (navy/gold and navy/light-blue/white).

All garment branding uses the full stacked logo. Use of any logo on clothing — and particularly linear logos — requires prior design approval by the Brand Guardian.

## 16. Advertising

- All advertising and promotional materials **must follow the brand guidelines**.
- **Corporate advertising** is produced in accordance with the guidelines by **ASL Corporate Affairs**.
- All airline and company **sales and marketing advertising and promotions** must comply with the brand guidelines — confirm with the Brand Guardian when in doubt.
- ASL Corporate Affairs and the Brand Guardian are available to assist with requests for brand advertising and promotions.

**Standard layout principles** (from the source examples):

- Logo top-right.
- Large product / aircraft photograph occupying most of the top half.
- Headline in white on a translucent ASL-Dark-Blue panel inside the image.
- Body copy on an ASL Dark Blue panel below.
- **Tagline:** "**ASL Airlines** Commitment. Integrity. Vision."
- Country / airline logo strip across the foot of the advert.

Two sample headlines from the guide:
- "Our customers are happy when we put our nose in their business" (B747-400 nose loading)
- "You may not have flown with us, but your packages probably have!" (B737 freighter)

These illustrate the brand voice: **confident, slightly playful, customer-focused, emphasising the express-parcel and e-commerce mission**.
